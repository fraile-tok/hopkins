The World's Thy Ship

J.P. Iglesias

   For the girl of Lisieux

The world's thy ship,

not thy destination,

Yet you cannot skip

to God's green nation.

The world's no grave,

nor small, cold cell,

but you must be brave

or drown on its swell.

The world's a ship,

not mere coffin, prais!

After this strange trip,

comes God's embrace.

    Here, Man: sail joyful this way,

    till the glorious dawn of such sweet day.
