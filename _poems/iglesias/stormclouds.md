---
title: Stormclouds
author: J.P. Iglesias
---

As the black stormclouds loom,
roused to being by whatever
foulness floats in the air.
I watch from afar,
awry of what there is to say.
Long, grey fingers spurt out
of the slow-moving masses,
casting darkness over the remnants
of what the Sun shone over
earlier that day.

Rain comes. Drizzle at first.
We rush to shelter as the least 
and last of the sky's orange hues
fade under the weight of night.
There's a flash in the sky.
It comes to a crash.
The ground trembles.
Our feet catch the tremors
as another comes down,
with more might and more bright.

"What will you tell the children?"
you ask, talking about a life
yet to be with fright in your eyes.
"That storms often come
but always go.
"That the green of grass
comes from water's life.
"That the world is loved
even on days of storm."
