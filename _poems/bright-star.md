---
title: Bright star, would I were stedfast as thou art
author: John Keats
---

Bright star, would I were stedfast as thou art—
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Not in lone splendour hung aloft the night
And watching, with eternal lids apart,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Like nature's patient, sleepless Eremite,
The moving waters at their priestlike task
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of pure ablution round earth's human shores,
Or gazing on the new soft-fallen mask
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of snow upon the mountains and the moors—
No—yet still stedfast, still unchangeable,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pillow'd upon my fair love's ripening breast,
To feel for ever its soft fall and swell,
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Awake for ever in a sweet unrest,
Still, still to hear her tender-taken breath,
And so live ever—or else swoon to death.
