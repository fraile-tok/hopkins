---
title: Pied Beauty
author: Gerard Manley Hopkins
---
Glory be to God for dappled things –
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For skies of couple-colour as a brinded cow;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For rose-moles all in stipple upon trout that swim;
Fresh-firecoal chestnut-falls; finches’ wings;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Landscape plotted and pieced – fold, fallow, and plough;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And áll trádes, their gear and tackle and trim.

All things counter, original, spare, strange;
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whatever is fickle, freckled (who knows how?)
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With swift, slow; sweet, sour; adazzle, dim;
He fathers-forth whose beauty is past change:
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Praise Him.
